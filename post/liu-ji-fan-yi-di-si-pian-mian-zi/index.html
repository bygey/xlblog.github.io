    • 六级翻译第四篇--面子

    "面子"是中国人际交往和商业往来中的典型现象.
    "mianzi" is a classical object of social and bussiness in China.

    Face is a typical phenomenon in Chinese interpersonal and commercial communication.

    "面子"在汉语中没有专门的定义,和"名声"的概念相近.

    There is not a special definite means about "mianzi" which is same as "notable".

    There is no special definition of "face" in Chinese, which is similar to the concept of "fame" .

    如果你不太熟悉中国文化,你应该了解一下"面子"或"有面子".

    Maybe you should learn about "mianzi" or "have mianzi" as you do not konw well about Chinese culture.

    If you are not familiar with Chinese culture, you should know something about "face" or "having face" .

    如果你不给别人"留面子",他们会认为你不尊重他们.

    If you don't give "mianzi" to others, they will think that you don't respect them.

    中国人生活的方方面面都有"面子"的影子.

    You can see the shadow of "mianzi" from everywhere in Chinese people's life.

    It is easy to find "face" in all aspects of Chinese life.

    中国人不喜欢说"不",因为他们想给双方"留面子",因此他们会说"现在不太方便","太难了"或"可能吧",但是不会对别人说一个"不"字.

    Chinese don't like to say "no" for they wanting to save "mianzi" for each other, so they may say "it is not easy now", "too difficult" or "maybe" but say a word of "no".

    Chinese people dislike to say the word "no", for they want to "save face" for both parties, so they may say "it is inconvenient for me right now", "it is too difficult" or "maybe", instead of merely saying "no " to others.
